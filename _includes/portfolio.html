Call of Duty Warzone

Working on Call of Duty: Warzone was undoubtedly one of the highlights of my career so far. The game’s first year live was an amazing time playing with friends after work and updating the game every day.

As the longest-serving and, at times, the sole UI engineer on Warzone 1, I was involved from prototyping all the way through to sunsetting, over a span of four years. This journey provided countless learning opportunities and was incredibly rewarding.

The Radix Endeavor

I thoroughly enjoyed working on this game, particularly because we programmed a functioning Mendelian evolution system that could be triggered mid-quest to have the world respond to player actions and scripted plot developments. This added a unique depth to the game. I tested, debugged, and fixed quests on top of my UI and Gameplay programming work.

This was also my first experience working on an MMO, making the network side of development incredibly fascinating. Initially, I was part of a two-person engineering team, but I eventually took the reins solo after launch, managing updates and maintenance.

This project provided my first opportunity to program an inventory GUI. It was a cool challenge to create something I had used for decades in games like WoW and other titles. More Details
